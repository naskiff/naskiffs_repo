# Vorrog Bar Ghash

Your are sitting on the grass and waiting, meditative...  You look to the side and see your dwarven brothers, wearing the same robes as you.

You look down to your stubby legs, before reaching for your beard, stroking it down with your broad dwarven hand, exhaling away all the pressure on your mind that remained...

It's almost time to begin. You sit properly and get ready.

You feel the energy change around you, sensing that your brothers are getting ready.

You gaze one last time into the distance, to admire the familiar scenery...
Down the hill are lit roads and buildings, with lights shaded pink, green, orange.
The lake rests further behind, undisturbed.
The mist, ever present, feels like a being of its own, older and wiser than all of us. It embraces the town, the endless forest around you, and the plain on which your brothers are gathered...

And up there, in the sky, is the Heart of Arawn, the Eternal King of Annwn.

The Heart is a giant floating ball, pulsating with power.  It expands violently, breaking into countless black shards to soak the world into a pale pink light, before contracting lazily until the shards reform into a ball, to leave just a faint, blood red glow.

Your brothers begin chanting, "Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
You join them, gazing into the Heart of Arawn, welcoming the trance...
"Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
This is our last hope.

And you repeat, "Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
You chant to the ceiling, maybe to convince it,
"Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
You grab the straw that you have been sleeping in,
"Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
Your lids finally close after minutes of staring, the burn in your eyes comforting...

And you remember, "Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
You feel the misery, the hopelessness...
"Find Aeron... Save the Kuun..."
And the solemn anger, your immediate duty!

You get up! It's the middle of the night. You put on your clothes, and go.
You slide the heavy slab that serves as door to your hovel, with the difficulty that comes with an old goblin's age.  You rest your back on the slab, to catch one breath.
You remember, you need to find "Aeron". You need to save the "Kuun".

Feeling resolved and solemn, you walk to the gate. Stone, mortar, impressive. The doors are kept open at night. You wander out. You know you need to go into the forest.

As you breach into the unknown, the unsafe, you feel a brisk, childish whimper, which you tame out right away, resolve returning.  Reason returning...  You have lived so long already, you are so tired. Why have you not thought of this before?
This is a good way to go, this is a good time to die.

You give a sad defeated smile to yourself, observing your own humiliation; a frail old goblin, caring for the kennels of a corrupt, vicious Lord. A goblin who has lost his mind, now wandering towards his end just for a fever dream.

It's quiet. You avoid tripping on overgrown roots. You barely dodge a spider web.
"Is this entertaining to the gnomes?" You joke to yourself.
You could just sit here and wait for them to come and end you.

"No." You push onwards, the solemn anger coming back.
"Show yourself, Aeron..." You tilt your head down, but focus forward, wrestling against your old bones.

You made it to a clearing, and there is a lake. You've finally arrived. You know it. There is a man, you think, walking towards the lake in the distance.  He stumbles to his knees, but he forces himself to walk again, towards the lake.

You increase your speed.  You walk faster, but it's not fast enough - you jog. He is wearing a full set of plate armor, but he has no head, and he is still struggling ahead. Your steps are noisy, and he notices you and turns to face you.  You keep jogging, showing your clear intent, you came for him, you came to save him!

He stumbles to his knees again, in pain, and two white wolves jump out of his chest and lunge at you.
-Death, finally-

You exhale, at peace, ready to lose yourself. You welcome the pain, you know it will be quick.

The wolves jump out of the knight's chest, you hear their paws tap once onto the forest floor, before they lunge at you, straight into your heart!

You feel the two bites on your heart, and wince hard! You squeeze your fist to your chest, your eyes forced shut!... And you endure...  Until the pain begins to fade...

You glance at the headless knight, and you see a red flame where his neck should be - blood red like the Heart of Arawn - and it fades...  and he slumps forward lifelessly.

He begins to split into black shards in front of your eyes, just like the Heart...  But there is no reconstructing.  They split into small shards, and disappear.

...

You walk to the lake, kneel in front of it, and cup water to your face...  You take a big breath, and begin to rest, taking in the beautiful sight of the sparkly lake, illuminated by the moon.

Your journey hasn't ended...
You slump back onto the ground behind you, your arms and legs spread out, completely spent.
Your consciousness begins to fade...  You are exhausted, and you need to sleep...  Because...

Arawn, the King of Annwn, has tasked you with bringing his Kuun back to him, in Annwn.
